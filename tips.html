Tips for Better Diabetes Management

Nutrition and Eating Habits

  • Eat balanced meals with fiber, protein, and healthy fats to slow blood sugar spikes.
  • Watch carbohydrate portions, especially refined carbs like white bread, sweets, and sugary drinks.
  • Choose high-fiber foods such as vegetables, legumes, whole grains, and berries.
  • Eat at regular times to help keep blood sugar levels steady.
  • Limit sugary beverages and opt for water, unsweetened tea, or sparkling water.
  • Read food labels to spot hidden sugars and understand carb content.

Physical Activity

  • Move your body regularly, aiming for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week.
  • Include strength training to improve insulin sensitivity.
  • Avoid long periods of sitting; short walks after meals can help lower glucose levels.
  • Choose activities you enjoy so exercise stays sustainable.

Monitoring and Medical Care

  • Check blood sugar as recommended and track patterns, not just single numbers.
  • Take medications or insulin exactly as prescribed.
  • Attend regular checkups, including A1C tests, eye exams, and foot checks.
  • Ask questions and speak up if something is not working for you.

Weight and Heart Health

  • Aim for a healthy weight; even modest weight loss can improve blood sugar control.
  • Manage blood pressure and cholesterol, which are closely linked to diabetes outcomes.
  • Limit alcohol and never drink on an empty stomach.

Stress, Sleep and Mental Health

  • Manage stress with walking, breathing exercises, meditation, or hobbies.
  • Get consistent quality sleep, since poor sleep can raise blood sugar.
  • Pay attention to mental health; burnout and diabetes distress are common.
  • Seek support from healthcare providers, support groups, friends, or family.

Daily Safety Habits

  • Take care of your feet by checking daily and wearing well-fitting shoes.
  • Stay hydrated; dehydration can raise blood sugar.
  • Have a plan for sick days, as illness can affect glucose levels.
